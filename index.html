Words, Lines, and Such...

A Collection of Poetic Fragments by Jaimie Richmond

Not Mine Dream The Outbreak

Not Mine

This is not mine
And that’s okay
The joy
The warmth
The love
The gift
It belongs to someone else

This hope is not mine
And that’s okay
The plan
the wish
The words
The bond
It belongs to someone else

This blessing is not mine
And that’s okay
The want
The question
The receipt
The relief
It belongs to someone else

Mine is the grief
And that’s okay
Remembering
Praying
Crying
Knowing
It belongs to me
Wishing
Wondering
Longing
Reminding
It belongs to me

Mine is the joy
That comes from God
Peace
Love
Grace
Comfort
It is enough for me

Dream

There’s, upon relinquishing mind’s duty,
Time — enough even to make stay
Things we care for somewhat less: the beauty
Of a child, with his innocence maintained.

However, upon waking from this lie,
The time we had now suddenly is gone,
And just the subtle blinking of an eye
Becomes an effort which takes far too long.

Awake, there is far less than time enough
For what is necessary — and I fear,
While contemplating such an unjust loss,
The hour melts away into a year.

Dreams such as these — difficult to resist,
Where one day takes a lifetime to complete.
For, when I wake, my life is but a mist;
A moment from now, I am obsolete.

The Outbreak

She waits,
watching for them.
But they do not come.

Where are they?
They always arrive —
but today, they do not.
And tomorrow, they do not.

What would you do,
if you were her?
It would be easy to leave.
But she doesn’t.
She stands,
and waits.
And she prepares a path forward.

For she knows,
someday,
they will arrive…
And we will all continue forward,
and her wait will have been worthwhile.

So she waits
Moments
Days
Years even
And keeps her faith
In this system
That at first glance
Looks so flawed.
She stands her ground.

And things go on
Eventually
Alien faces appear
But they do not return
But they must soon
And so she waits

Faces melt into normalcy
Gradually
And everything returns to normalcy
Gradually
But not really

For some things
Cannot be taken back
But still I wait
And I fight
For what I, what she, once had
