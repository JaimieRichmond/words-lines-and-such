A Collection of Poems by Jaimie Richmond

Shadows

Beating

Thumping, thumping --
You're here beside me.
Although I long for peace,
I do love the thumping.

And there's no denying
Your noiseful small findings;
For to not hear this sound
Would be much like dying.

Beating, beating --
After which I'm pining.
And I shall forfeit peace
To watch your heart beating.

Noticing you thinking
Gives to me an inkling
That something more profound
Is in those eyes, twinkling.

Thudding, thudding --
Sometimes you are crying.
Oh, how I loathe this peace!
Alongside the thudding.

It leaves me resigning,
And hoping, and sighing,
Because watching you frown
Is nothing but trying.

Thumping, thumping --
New eyes are now shining.
Something strange, little piece
Of someone else thumping.

Beating

This sound redefining,
A new joy I'm finding.
With each little, soft pound,
It's hard to keep hiding.

Beating, beating --
Within me he's thriving.
I listen to his sound...
This new little beating.

The beating grows louder
Within each passing hour.
For this new love I've found,
Surely nothing could sour.

Then comes pause
In the thudding.

Remembering how I forgot
That a sound is most deafening
When suddenly it's not.

But you are here, thudding,
My original lot.
Your beating and thumping,
And the joy it once brought...

Yet now your soft beating
Brings some joy to my ears --
Joy hidden in the shadow
Of what I no longer hear.
